<p><p>The general-purpose resistors market is divided primarily into SMD (Surface Mount Device) and through-hole types. SMD resistors are compact components designed for high-density circuits, allowing for automated assembly and efficient space usage, making them popular in modern electronics. Through-hole resistors, on the other hand, feature leads that are inserted into holes on a PCB, providing robust connections but requiring more space and manual assembly, often preferred in high-power applications or prototyping where durability is essential.</p></p>
