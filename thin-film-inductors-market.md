<p><p>The thin film inductors market is primarily divided into two types: SMD (Surface-Mount Device) and plug-in types. SMD inductors are compact, designed for surface mounting on PCBs, offering high reliability and efficiency, making them ideal for modern electronic applications with space constraints. Conversely, plug-in inductors are larger, typically inserted into sockets, providing flexibility for maintenance and adjustments. Both types cater to different applications, with SMDs favored for high-density applications and plug-ins for ease of use in prototyping and repairs.</p></p>
