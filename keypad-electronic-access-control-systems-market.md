<p><p>Keypad electronic access control systems are categorized into two main types: networked systems and standalone systems. Networked systems integrate with broader security frameworks, allowing centralized management and real-time monitoring across multiple access points, ideal for larger facilities. In contrast, standalone systems operate independently at single access points, offering simplicity and ease of installation, perfect for smaller sites or specific areas. Both types enhance security by requiring users to input a unique code, ensuring controlled access to restricted areas.</p></p>
