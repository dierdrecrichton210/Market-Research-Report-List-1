<p><p>Cognac is classified into several market categories based on aging: VS (Very Special) is aged for a minimum of two years, offering a fresh and vibrant flavor. VSOP (Very Superior Old Pale) is aged for at least four years, providing a smoother, more complex profile. XO (Extra Old) requires a minimum of ten years of aging, resulting in rich and deep flavors. These classifications help consumers navigate quality and taste, with older Cognacs typically being more premium and expensive.</p></p>
